John Madden

John Madden is meaningful to me because he changed football forever—from the way the game is played, to how it is taught, to how millions of fans understand it. As a Super Bowl–winning coach, a groundbreaking broadcaster, and the face of one of the most influential sports video game franchises ever created, Madden made football accessible to everyone. His passion, energy, and ability to explain the game shaped generations of players and fans, including me.

Timeline of John Madden’s Life

  • 1936 – Born in Austin, Minnesota
  • 1958 – Drafted by the Philadelphia Eagles (career cut short by injury)
  • 1969 – Became head coach of the Oakland Raiders
  • 1977 – Won Super Bowl XI with the Raiders
  • 1979 – Retired from coaching with a .759 winning percentage
  • 1980s–2000s – Became one of the most iconic NFL broadcasters in history
  • 1988 – The first Madden NFL video game was released
  • 2006 – Inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame
  • 2021 – Passed away at age 85
